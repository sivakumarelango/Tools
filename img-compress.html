Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our image compression tool

Is there a limit to the image size I can compress? +

There's no fixed limit, but very large images (over 10MB) may take longer to process depending on your device's capabilities. For best results, we recommend compressing images under 20MB.

Will compressing my image reduce its quality? +

Some quality reduction is inevitable when compressing, but our tool finds the optimal balance between file size and visual quality. At higher compression levels (70-100), the difference is often imperceptible to the human eye.

What's the difference between the output formats? +

JPEG is best for photographs, PNG for graphics with transparency, and WebP offers superior compression for both. Our "Auto" option analyzes your image and selects the most efficient format automatically.

Are my images stored on your servers? +

No, all processing happens in your browser. Your images never leave your device, ensuring complete privacy and security. We don't store or have access to any of your files.

Which format gives the smallest file size? +

WebP typically offers the best compression (25-35% smaller than JPEG at similar quality). However, for maximum compatibility, JPEG is still the most widely supported format across all platforms.
